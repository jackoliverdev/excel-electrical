AI Chat Widget Developer Specification
Purpose
Create an AI chat widget for the website that helps visitors quickly identify the type of work they need, collect the
key enquiry details, review any uploaded attachments where possible, and pass a clear summary to the correct
human contact via WhatsApp for follow-up.
The widget should support both AI-led enquiry handling and human handover through WhatsApp during working
hours or out of hours.

1. Initial Widget Behaviour
When the widget opens for the first time, it should display a friendly welcome message and ask the visitor to
choose one of the following:
First Selection
1. Domestic Works
2. Commercial Works
This selection must be stored and used to route the customer into the correct journey.
Example welcome message:
Hi, thanks for getting in touch. Are you enquiring about domestic or commercial works today?
Once the customer makes this selection, the widget should move into either the Domestic Works Journey or the
Commercial Works Journey below.

2. Domestic Works Journey
This journey is for homeowners, landlords, tenants, residential property managers, and anyone enquiring about
works at a house, flat, apartment, rental property, domestic garage, driveway, or residential building.
Domestic Main Scope Options
After selecting Domestic Works, the widget should ask:
Thanks. What type of domestic work can we help you with?
Options:
1. Electrical Works
2. Fire Safety
3. Access &amp; Security
4. Garage Doors &amp; Electric Gates
5. EV Charging

2.1 Domestic Electrical Works
Sub-options:
1. Fault finding &amp; repairs

2. New lights, sockets &amp; upgrades
3. Consumer Unit / fuse box maintenance and replacement
4. Electrical safety checks / EICR
5. Electrical installation certification / EICs
6. Other domestic electrical work
Suggested follow-up questions:
 Is this an urgent electrical fault or a planned job?
 Can you briefly describe the issue or work required?
 Which part of the property is affected?
 Is there any loss of power, burning smell, sparking, or repeated tripping?
 Have you had any recent electrical work carried out?
 Is the property owned, rented, or managed on behalf of someone else?
 For EICR or certification enquiries: is this for a landlord, homeowner, house sale, rental, insurance, or
compliance requirement?

4. Attachment Upload Option
The widget must include an attachment option so the customer can upload relevant files for the AI bot and human
team to review.
Attachment Types
The attachment feature should support, where technically possible:
1. Images
2. Videos
3. Documents
4. PDFs
5. Certificates
6. Electrical reports
7. Fire alarm reports
8. EICR reports
9. Photos of faults, panels, consumer units, gates, chargers, alarms, or access systems
10. Short video clips showing the issue

Attachment Prompt
The bot should ask:
Do you have any photos, videos, documents, reports, or certificates you would like to attach? These can
help us understand the issue before we contact you.
Options:
1. Yes, upload attachments
2. No attachments
3. I can send them later
AI Review Requirement
Where technically possible, the AI bot should be able to read and review uploaded attachments to extract useful
context, such as:
 Visible fault details
 Photos of consumer units, distribution boards, chargers, alarms, gates, garage doors, access panels, or
wiring
 Existing certificate or report details
 EICR observations or remedial codes
 Fire alarm panel faults
 Manufacturer or model information
 Site photos showing access, parking, or installation location
 Safety concerns visible in images or videos
The AI should summarise any useful findings from attachments in the WhatsApp handover message.
Important note for developer:
The AI should not make final safety-critical decisions based only on images or attachments. Any potential safety
issue should be flagged for human review.
Attachment Storage / Handover Requirement
Uploaded attachments should either be:
1. Sent directly through the WhatsApp handover where possible, or
2. Stored securely and linked in the WhatsApp summary for the team to review.
The WhatsApp handover should include:
Attachments Uploaded: Yes / No / Sending later
Attachment Types: Images / Videos / Documents / PDFs / Certificates / Reports
Attachment Links: [Secure links if applicable]
AI Attachment Summary: [Short summary of useful findings]
Safety Concerns Flagged From Attachments: Yes / No / Unsure

5. Universal Intake Questions
After the work category and sub-section have been selected, the widget should collect the following details.
Customer Details
1. Full name
2. Company name, if commercial

3. Contact telephone number
4. Email address
5. Full site address, including postcode
6. Preferred contact method: phone, WhatsApp, or email
7. Best time to contact
Job Details
1. Is the enquiry urgent?
2. Is there a safety concern, loss of power, active fault, alarm issue, access issue, or system failure?
3. Is the work required at a home, rental property, business premises, communal building, or other site?
4. Is this a new installation, repair, upgrade, inspection, certification, or maintenance enquiry?
5. Please provide a short description of the work required.
6. Are there any photos, videos, reports, certificates, or documents available to upload?
7. Is there a preferred date or deadline?
8. Is access available to the property or site?
9. Is parking available nearby?
10. Does the customer need a quotation?
Quotation Question
The widget should ask:
Would you like us to provide a quotation for this work?
Options:
1. Yes, I need a quotation
2. No, I need urgent help / a call back
3. I’m not sure yet

6. Urgency Logic
The widget should identify urgent enquiries and escalate them more clearly.
Urgent Trigger Examples
 No power
 Burning smell
 Sparks
 Electrical fault
 Tripping electrics
 Fire alarm fault
 Security/access system failure
 Gate or garage door stuck open or closed
 EV charger fault causing electrical concern
 Commercial compliance deadline
 Safety concern
 Uploaded image/video appears to show damage, exposed wiring, overheating, burning, water ingress,
broken equipment, or blocked access
If urgency is detected, the bot should respond with:
Thanks. This sounds like it may need urgent attention. I’ll collect the key details now and send them
straight through so someone can review this as soon as possible.

For serious immediate danger, the bot should also state:
If there is immediate danger, fire, smoke, or risk to life, please call the emergency services immediately.

7. AI Bot Conversation Flow
Recommended flow:
1. Welcome message
2. Select Domestic Works or Commercial Works
3. Route to the correct Domestic or Commercial journey
4. Select main scope of work
5. Select sub-section
6. Ask relevant job-specific questions
7. Ask universal customer details
8. Ask if a quotation is required
9. Offer attachment upload for images, videos, documents, reports, and certificates
10. AI reviews uploaded attachments where technically possible
11. Confirm preferred contact method
12. Show the customer a short summary
13. Ask the customer to confirm the details are correct
14. Send summary and attachment details to WhatsApp contact for human follow-up
15. Display final confirmation message
Example final customer message:
Thanks, we’ve received your enquiry and passed the details to the relevant person. Someone will review
this and contact you shortly using your preferred contact method.

nominated WhatsApp number.

9. Working Hours and Out of Hours Logic
The widget should be able to tag the enquiry as either:
1. During working hours
2. Out of hours
The WhatsApp message should include this status.
Example:
Received: Out of hours
Suggested behaviour:
 During working hours: send the enquiry to the relevant person/team for normal follow-up.
 Out of hours: still send the WhatsApp summary, clearly marked as out of hours.
 Urgent out-of-hours enquiries should be marked clearly as urgent.
Example urgent out-of-hours heading:
URGENT OUT OF HOURS WEBSITE ENQUIRY

10. Human Handover Trigger

The bot should offer human handover when:
 The customer asks to speak to someone
 The enquiry is urgent
 The customer provides unclear or complex information
 The uploaded attachment shows or suggests a possible safety concern
 The work involves commercial compliance
 The customer reports a safety concern
 The customer needs a quotation or site visit
 The bot cannot confidently categorise the enquiry
Example handover message:
Thanks, I’ll pass this to a member of the team so they can review it properly and contact you directly.

11. Suggested Widget Design Notes
The widget should feel simple, guided, and professional.
Recommended design approach:
 Use buttons for Domestic / Commercial selection.
 Once selected, keep the journey clearly labelled as either Domestic Works or Commercial Works.
 Use buttons or cards for the five main scopes.
 Use buttons for sub-sections.
 Use free-text fields only when the customer needs to describe the issue.
 Use short question steps instead of one long form.
 Include an attachment upload step for images, videos, documents, certificates, and reports.
 Clearly show uploaded file names before submission.
 Allow customers to remove or replace attachments before submitting.
 Allow customers to go back and change previous answers.
 Display a final summary before submission.
 Include consent wording before sending customer details and attachments via WhatsApp or secure
handover link.
Suggested consent wording:
By submitting this enquiry, you agree for your details and any uploaded attachments to be shared with our
team so we can contact you about your request.
